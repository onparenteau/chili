Types of Cats!

Hybrid Lynx

Lynx hybrids, also known as "lynxcats" or "blynx", are the offspring of bobcats and Canada lynx. They have larger bodies, smaller feet, and some lynx-like features, like long ear tufts and black-tipped tails. However, the hybrids are not protected and may be hunted by hunters. Some odd-looking lynx may be color morphs of either bobcats or Canada lynx, which could lead to the death of protected Canada lynx. My parents have had two hybrid lynx cats. The first one was named Titus and the second one is named Mika. Mika is now 3 years old and shes so pretty and loves to explore outside.

Siamese Cat

The Siamese cat is one of the first distinctly recognised breeds of Asian cat. Derived from the Wichianmat landrace, one of several varieties of cats native to Thailand, the original Siamese became one of the most popular breeds in Europe and North America in the 19th century.

Bangal Cat

The Bengal cat is a breed of hybrid cat created from crossing of an Asian leopard cat, with domestic cats, especially the spotted Egyptian Mau. My boyfriend had a bangal cat named Cash. Cash was super sweet and waited for him to get home everynight from work to play.

Ragdoll Cats

The Ragdoll is a breed of cat with a distinct colorpoint coat and blue eyes. Its morphology is large and weighty, and it has a semi-long and silky soft coat. American breeder Ann Baker developed Ragdolls in the 1960s. They are best known for their docile, placid temperament and affectionate nature.

Main Coon

The Maine Coon is a large domesticated cat breed. It is one of the oldest natural breeds in North America. The breed originated in the U.S. state of Maine, where it is the official state cat.
